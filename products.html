Conventional Vs Organic

Our solution works for both conventional and organic farmers. However, conventional farmers should apply these products separately and avoid mixing the two sources.

Fertilizers

We produce two kinds of fertilizers: solid and liquid. Each type has its own advantages, depending on factors like farm conditions and specific needs. Sometimes, using both types together can offer the best results.

Liquid Organic Fertilizer

Our liquid organic fertilizer addresses various issues, such as:

  • Enhancing photosynthesis in beans and rice.
  • Promoting resilience in crops during heavy rains and hot weather.
  • Improving fruiting and branching in plants like groundnuts and beans.
  • Preventing flower and fruit abortion.

Application

Apply 1ml/L, spraying both sides of the leaf and on the soil surface around the stem. Best applied early in the morning or late in the evening.

Frequency

Once per week for heavily depleted soils, or twice a week during pest outbreaks to break the reproductive cycle.

Storage and Handling

Our organic fertilizer should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. It should be handled with care to avoid contamination and stored in a sealed container to prevent odors and pests.
